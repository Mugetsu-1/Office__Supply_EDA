### Results and Findings

The dataset was successfully cleaned by handling missing values, correcting data types, and removing inconsistencies to ensure reliable analysis.

Revenue analysis showed that a small subset of products contributes a large portion of total revenue, indicating an uneven revenue distribution across product categories.

Regional analysis revealed noticeable differences in sales performance between regions, with certain regions consistently generating higher revenue than others.

Product-level analysis identified top-performing products as well as low-performing items, highlighting opportunities for inventory optimization and strategic focus.

Price distribution analysis showed variation in product pricing, with most products concentrated within a moderate price range and fewer high-priced outliers.

Time-based analysis (monthly trends) indicated seasonality in sales, with identifiable peak and off-peak periods throughout the year.

Correlation analysis demonstrated relationships between numerical features such as quantity sold, revenue, and price, helping understand which factors most influence sales performance.

Pareto analysis (80/20 rule) confirmed that approximately 20% of products account for around 80% of total revenue, reinforcing the importance of focusing on high-impact products.

Sales representative analysis showed performance variation among sales reps, suggesting differences in regional demand, product focus, or sales efficiency.

### Overall Insight

The EDA highlights clear patterns in revenue concentration, regional performance, and seasonality. The results emphasize the importance of data-driven decision-making for product prioritization, regional strategy, and sales planning. This project demonstrates practical application of EDA techniques for extracting meaningful insights from business-oriented datasets.
